About the Phonograph

When was the tool or technology developed/invented?

The phonograph was invented in 1877. This device could record your voice by making a needle vibrate. The sound vibrations would go through the needle and make a groove into tin foil. A needle on the other side could play back what you had just recorded. This is the earliest form of record player.

What need did it address?

The phonograph addressed the need for a music recorder and player. People could now make listen to whatever music they wanted, when they wanted, where they wanted, and for as long as they wanted. People began listening to music differently, people could now analyze lyrics in depth. The phonograph was also instrumental in the development of jazz.

Who developed/invented it?

The famous inventor Thomas Edison invented the phonograph. He also invented some of the most essential pieces of technology like the incandescent light bulb, alkaline battery, and one of the earliest motion picture cameras. He held a world-record for his 1,093 patents. He also improved the telegraph and telephone.

What advance did it make possible?

This advance made it possible to record and listen to music for the first time in history. Instead of having to go to a concert or play by hand, it allowed you to be in your own home and listen to music or record your own for later enjoyment, but the quality was very poor.

One of the earliest surviving recordings of music

1888 recording of Arthur Sullivan's "The Lost Chord", recorded by George Gouraud, and played at the August 14, 1888, press conference that introduced the phonograph to London.

How has the tool or technology been developed, improved, or expanded beyond its original incarnation?

The phonograph made way for record players on disks which would then go on to be developed into CDs and DVDs. The basic premise about grooves on a rotating object disk has remained the same for over a century. Record players use a disk and a needle and CDs and DVDs use a disk and lasers to get obtain signals. The only thing which has change is the technology and the materials used to create such devices.

What simple machines is it made up of?

The main simple machine in the phonograph is the wheel and axel. It is a cylinder that is engraved that is used to play back sounds. There is also a lever for the crank handle. There are also screws used to build the device.
